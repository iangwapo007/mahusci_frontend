Cell Structure

In this lesson, we'll explore the basic structure of cells, the fundamental building blocks of all living organisms. Understanding cell structure is essential for comprehending how biological systems function at their most basic level.

Key Concepts

  • Cells are the basic structural and functional units of all living organisms
  • All cells are surrounded by a cell membrane that regulates what enters and leaves the cell
  • Prokaryotic cells lack a nucleus, while eukaryotic cells have a nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles
  • Plant cells have cell walls and chloroplasts, which animal cells lack

Cell Organelles and Their Functions

Each organelle in a eukaryotic cell performs a specific function that contributes to the overall function of the cell:

  • Nucleus: Contains genetic material (DNA) and controls cellular activities
  • Mitochondria: Produces energy (ATP) through cellular respiration
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum: Synthesizes and transports proteins and lipids
  • Golgi Apparatus: Modifies, sorts, and packages proteins for secretion
  • Lysosomes: Contain digestive enzymes for breaking down cellular waste
  • Chloroplasts: (In plant cells) Site of photosynthesis

In the next lesson, we'll explore how these structures work together to perform essential cellular functions.
