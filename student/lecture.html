Cell Structure Lesson

You’re probably familiar with the breathtaking Sierra Madre, the longest mountain range in the Philippines

Key Concepts
✓
Cells are the basic structural and functional units of all living organisms
✓
All cells are surrounded by cell membrane that regulates what enters and leaves the cell
✓
Prokaryotic cells lack a nucleus, while eukaryotic cells have a nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles
✓
Plant cells have cell walls and chloroplasts, which animal cells lack
Cell Organelles and Their Functions

Each organelle in a eukaryotic cell performs a specific function that contributes to the overall function of the cell:

Nucleus
Contains genetic material (DNA) and controls cellular activities
Mitochondria
Produces energy (ATP) through cellular respiration
Endoplasmic Reticulum
Synthesizes and transports proteins and lipids
Golgi Apparatus
Modifies, sorts, and packages proteins for secretion
Lysosomes
Contain digestive enzymes for breaking down cellular waste
Chloroplasts
(In plant cells) Site of photosynthesis

In the next lesson, we'll explore how these structures work together to perform essential cellular functions.
